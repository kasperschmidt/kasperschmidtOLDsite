Cox... you can still make up for it!

 

Internet at home still pending.

 

Back to work. As you can see above I have already managed to make my office cozy - kind of. Even though the office is in the middle of the building so there are no windows to outside I actually like it - then I can concentrate on what I’m there for. But besides starting to get up to speed on new projects I have nothing special to report yet.

But our tv antennae arrived...
when I first unwrapped it I saw to my bitter surprise that it was a coax antenna, and as we shipped our danish tv over here it uses the european standard RV input. Hence I spent the weekend hunting down a coax-to-RV adaptor. I found one at Radioschack which costed basically the same as the antenna - but without it the antenna would be useless. Well either way, I got home and plugged in the antenna but unfortunately this didn’t result in us having more channels. We just have a better signal on ABC. It appears that ABC is the only channel we can catch the old-fashioned way here so no NFL at home for me:’( well then I just have to watch college football when I’m home and find a bar when I want to watch some NFL I guess.
With the antenna the new modem for our internet also arrived. But this didn’t go easy either. Last wednesday the Cox cable guy showed up while I was at work and (supposedly) turned on the internet connection.
When I got the modem last week I tried to hook it up but there was no signal what-so-ever, so friday morning I called Cox to hear if they could please send out another cable guy to make sure everything was fine. At first the guy I talked with was kind of cocky and just said ‘do this’ and ‘do that’. However, as I’m not a complete idiot with electronics (if I have to say myself ;) I had already tried his solutions and he finally arrived at the same conclusion as me, namely that there is no signal. At first they could only send out a guy tomorrow, i.e., 5 days after I talked with them, but he called me back after two hours and said he scheduled a guy for sunday evening.
Sweet - nice guy to actually ask them to pass by on a sunday to fix it... well it was indeed too good to be true. I was home waiting for the cable guy but he never showed up so I’m still without (our own - the landlord has been so kind to lend us his wireless until we get ours fixed) internet at home. When I called them they couldn’t help me (this time the guy I talked to was not even trying to be helpful) and all they could see was that I had an appointment tomorrow. So now I just hope they can fix it tomorrow as promised - you can still make up for you incompetence Cox Cable, but my patience is running out...
Well enough bad vibes for this time! Besides finding and adapter to the tv-antenna I went to Santa Barbara Farmers Market this saturday, and damn that’s great. I’m pretty sure we will spent quite some tuesdays and saturdays stocking up on fresh vegetables here. One has to watch out, but in-between there are actually some really good deals.
And rumor has it that they sell very cheap shellfish sometimes - gotta look out for that!

Sunday Anna had invited some people to a BBQ at Goleta Beach Park to celebrate her birthday. Pretty neat to meet some more people and a great occasion to hang out with some of my new colleagues. The sky was clear, the beer cold, the ribs awesome, and the burgers great. Combining that with several ‘games’ and a bit of football-trowing makes for a great sunday (besides the fact I had to leave early just to get home to wait for a cable guy that never showed up... but enough said about that). I’m already looking forward to the next one!

Yesterday we were a couple of people who met to play some
(Taiwanese apparently - I didn’t know there was a difference before we had to refresh our memory regarding the rules yesterday) Mahjong. We learned it a couple of weeks ago by Sherry and her mom. Unfortunately Sherry left the department last week so now we are on our own. It’s a pretty cool game even though we haven’t explored it’s full potential yet - for instance we have no clue about the point/scoring system yet... but it’s not the last time we have played for sure!

And one last (but certainly not least) thing before it’s bedtime: Mette is arriving tomorrow evening. Brilliant!!


See you around.
